Dos & Don’ts of Owning a Dog

Responsible ownership starts with knowing what to do — and what to avoid.

Dos

  • Provide daily exercise and mental stimulation.
  • Train with positive reinforcement.
  • Keep vaccinations and vet visits up to date.
  • Socialize your dog with people and other animals.
  • Give affection, attention, and a safe environment.

Don’ts

  • Don’t leave dogs alone for long periods — they need companionship.
  • Don’t feed harmful foods or overuse treats.
  • Don’t use harsh punishment — it damages trust.
  • Don’t neglect grooming, dental care, or parasite prevention.
  • Don’t ignore behavioral issues — early training prevents bigger problems.

Responsible Ownership

Owning a dog is a lifelong commitment. It means providing love, healthcare, training, and a safe home. Dogs depend on us for everything — and in return, they give unconditional loyalty.
